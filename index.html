Introduction

The Regional Peace, Cohesion and Heritage Centre is a trust-based organization that is in Mandera County and serves the FCDC counties of Baringo, Garissa, Isiolo, Lamu, Mandera, Marsabit, Tana River, Samburu, Turkana, Wajir, and West Pokot along with Elgeyo Marakwet and areas around the Mandera triangle. The centre was established based on the recommendation by the Participatory Action Research (PAR) process implemented by the Mandera team to foster sustainable peace processes initiated by the NCIC/Interpeace peacebuilding programme.

The centre will use culture and local heritage as the bedrock of its engagements and aims to contribute to long-term peacebuilding initiatives through the provision of early warning to conflict, emergency responses, mediation, experiences sharing, and innovation as well as incubating best practices in conflict management and resolution. The centre serves as a unique library where Images for peacebuilding initiatives are available i.e., Audio-Visual materials, Research conducted on peace for the FCDC counties, and areas of the Mandera triangle.

Our Mandate

The Regional Peace, Cohesion, and Heritage Centre is to:

  • Provide daily conflict early warning and early response, data collection, verification, and analysis.

  • Aid conflict rapid response to the population in the Mandera triangle.

  • Offer training, guidance, and mentorship to the various peacebuilding Infrastructures.

  • Provide daily contact points for county officials and community interlocutors.

  • Coordinate all activities of grassroot structures like the Ceasefire Monitoring Committees (CMCs), Intervillage Dialogue Spaces etc.

  • Provide secretarial support to the Peace Actor's Forums.

  • Contribute to the efforts of the international community in crisis prevention and stability by offering secretarial support to IGAD's cross border Conflict Prevention, Resolution and Management (CPRM) cluster in the triangle (Kenya, Somalia, and Ethiopia)

  • Offer research, policy and learning opportunities that incubates local contextually appropriate peacebuilding and conflict management frameworks for pastoral communities.

  • Incubate best practices in conflict management that are contextually appropriate in pastoral conflicts. Offer an important knowledge reference learning centre for peacebuilding, and on issues around fragility and resilience in Kenya and the region. Be a central platform where Interpeace will share its over 25 years of experience in peacebuilding with the Mandera triangle regional administrations and other actors in the region.

Our Core Values

Advocacy

We advocate for peaceful coexistence and be a trusted arbiter to curb retaliation.

Community

We work with the community for peace initiatives and interventions for their betterment.

Integrity

We act with integrity and honesty in accordance with the highest professional and ethical standards.

Respect for Cultural Diversity

We are committed to recognise, respect, and appreciate the uniqueness of different customs and traditions to build unity and trust.

Innovation

Centre that will foster research knowledge on peace and conflict management.

Our Vision

A resilient, prosperous, and peaceful region where communities resolve conflict in a non-violent manner irrespective of cultural, ethnic, religious, and political differences

Our Mission

To empower the local populations to own, spearhead and implement peacebuilding initiatives through inclusive and participatory peace processes, cross-border conflict prevention, research, and audio-visual tools.

Strategic partners

  1. Peace Actors Forums (PAF)

  2. Local peacebuilding structures: ceasefire monitoring committees (CMCs), village dialogue space committees

  3. County and national governments (peace departments)

  4. National Steering Committee on Peacebuilding and Conflict Management (NSC)

  5. Frontier Counties Development Council (FCDC)

  6. Intergovernmental Authority on Development (IGAD)-Peace & Security Division- CEWARN & Mediation Support Unit

  7. Garissa University
