The Unveiling

Mandera County Governor, H.E Hon. Capt. Ali Ibrahim Roba during the unveiling of the Regional Peace, Cohesion, and Heritage Centre and the launch of the Cross-Border Health Report on 13th /12/2021 in Mandera County. He was joined by Inter peace Regional Director Dr Theo Hollander, County Commissioner Onesmus Kyatha, Deputy Governor H.E Mohamed Arai, Mandera County Assembly Speaker Hon. Mohamed Adan Khalif, IGAD representative Dr Aleo Garang, NCIC Commissioners and a team of staff from Interpeace led by Hassan Ismail.

Notes from the Speech;

Cross Border Communities

Roba said the centre will help cross border communities in Ethiopia, Somalia, and Kenya. He was grateful to the Almighty to see a project of this magnitude being commissioned in a land county donated

Peaceful and Secure Environment

A peaceful and secure environment is critical to every society since it affects all aspects of economic and social development in a country,” Roba said.

Peaceful Community

Elders from Banisa were recognized to have championed community peace in a sustainable manner by the Governor. He said peace has been sustained in the county through the input of elders even in election times when tensions rise. The governor thanked the Interpeace for equally playing a critical role in ensuring conflicts are resolved amicable with minimal violence and for funding the construction of the center with special mention of Hassan Ismail in spearheading this project.

Peaceful and security environment

A peaceful and secure environment is critical to every society since it affects all aspects of economic and social development in a country,” he said while thanking the county security team who have been swiftly responding to outbreaks of conflicts while working with leaders and communities to forestall sporadic violence.

Our History

2016: The concept of a locally based organization was birthed by networks from the following structures: Community, County Government, Working Group, and Technocrats (Finance & Accountants) who were born out of the NCIC-Interpeace peacebuilding programme who eventually became board members. To ground local ownership and sustainability of the peacebuilding, the concept of aa network to bring all this effort was conceived. The Network was to be a locally based organization that would co-implement, manage resources, and partner with organization working on peace matters was devised to have sustainable peacebuilding programmes in the region.

2019:Network for Peace, Cohesion and Heritage Trust (NEPCOH Trust) was registered. The Network collaborates, partners, and support a pool of peacebuilders and local peace infrastructures to undertake peacebuilding and development works in Kenya. The Network will further provide secretarial support to Peace Actors Forum in the Frontier Counties Development Council (FCDC) Counties and provide in-kind capacity support to formal and informal CBO, movements, and community initiatives involved in Peacebuilding and development work.

2021:On December 13, 2021, the Regional Peace Cohesion and Heritage Center in Mandera County was officially opened. H.E Hon. Capt. Ali Ibrahim Roba, Governor of Mandera County, commissioned the office. The Centre serve as the NEPCOH Trust's headquarters to coordinate all the country programmes.

2022:Network for Peace, Cohesion and Heritage Trust (NEPCOH Trust) officially started to roll out its programme as an independent entity to coordinate peacebuilding work of Kenya peacebuilding programme that co-implemented by NCIC and Interpeace. The Network helps in coordination of local peace infrastructures and building staff based at community levels. The Network also acts as a platform for advocacy to raise and amplify the voices of the population in the hotspot areas at the county, national and international levels and provide in-kind financial support to movements and community peacebuilding initiatives that do not have the financial systems to receive funding.
