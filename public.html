Our Mandate

The Regional Peace, Cohesion, and Heritage Centre is to:

  • ...Provide daily conflict early warning and early response, data collection, verification, and analysis.

  • ...Aid conflict rapid response to the population in the Mandera triangle.

  • ...Offer training, guidance, and mentorship to the various peacebuilding Infrastructures.

  • ...Provide daily contact points for county officials and community interlocutors.

  • ...Coordinate all activities of grassroot structures like the Ceasefire Monitoring Committees (CMCs), Intervillage Dialogue Spaces etc.

  • ...Provide secretarial support to the Peace Actor's Forums.

  • ...Contribute to the efforts of the international community in crisis prevention and stability by offering secretarial support to IGAD's cross border Conflict Prevention, Resolution and Management (CPRM) cluster in the triangle (Kenya, Somalia, and Ethiopia)

  • ...Offer research, policy and learning opportunities that incubates local contextually appropriate peacebuilding and conflict management frameworks for pastoral communities.

  • ...Incubate best practices in conflict management that are contextually appropriate in pastoral conflicts. Offer an important knowledge reference learning centre for peacebuilding, and on issues around fragility and resilience in Kenya and the region. Be a central platform where Interpeace will share its over 25 years of experience in peacebuilding with the Mandera triangle regional administrations and other actors in the region.

Specific Objectives

The Regional Peace, Cohesion, and Heritage Centre is to:

  • ...Carry out initiatives to promote peacebuilding, living together in dignity, good relations, harmony, and peaceful co-existence.

  • ...Foster more equitable sharing of power and resources, particularly in conflict-affected communities, and strengthen inclusive governance.

  • ...Manage conflict in non-violent and non-coercive ways.

  • ...Work in partnership with national and local actors to develop peace and cohesion.

  • ...Sustain social cohesion among communities with persistent historical and present grievances by fostering the 4-Rs (Reconciliation, Reintegration, Reconstruction, and Recovery)

  • ...Promote community development, peace projects, and other purposes beneficial to such people and areas facing violence, including training in peace and cohesion.

  • ...Build communities’ resilience to the effects of climate-related migration and conflict.

  • ...Coordinate, collaborate, compliment, and Synergize peacebuilding programmes with any other person, governmental or non-governmental organizations, and limit duplication.

  • ...Improve trust and collaborative relations between security actors and the public in target communities.

  • ...Build and expand a network of strong civil society and key personalities that contribute to effective peacebuilding efforts in Kenya and along its borders.

Organization Structure

Our Board

NEPCOH has a strong governance structure that consists of seasoned experts nominated to the position due to their experience in the various thematic areas implemented by the organization. The Board members are 7 in number and comprise 4 Representatives from grassroots Community peace structures, County Government, National government, and NGO, all working in the Peacebuilding sector, and 3 Technical Experts – Business community, Finance, Peacebuilding, and regional corporation. The Board is tasked with providing strategic direction, policy guidance, and professional support to the organization. It is key in defining NEPCOH Trust's overall policy and strategy and is the legal authority responsible for the organization.

Mahat Omar Board Secretary, NEPCOH Trust
